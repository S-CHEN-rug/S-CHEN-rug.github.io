Enhancing Surprise Perception in TTS through Keyword-Level Prosody Control

This project proposes a prosody control framework to enhance surprise in synthesized speech using FastSpeech2 and HiFi-GAN..
It is a master's thesis project for the MSc in Voice Technology at the University of Groningen.
Here are some pre-generated audio demos.

This study investigated whether enhancing prosodic cues—specifically pitch and energy—at emotionally salient keywords can improve the perception of surprise in synthesized speech. Using a customized FastSpeech2 and HiFi-GAN pipeline, the system selectively amplifies prosodic features only at targeted word regions, rather than applying global emotion conditioning.

The results from perception experiments show that listeners reliably identify surprise in the prosody-enhanced versions, and that the approach works across both English (a non-tonal language) and Mandarin Chinese (a tonal language). Interestingly, listener sensitivity to pitch manipulation differed across languages, suggesting that prosody control must account for linguistic constraints such as lexical tone.
